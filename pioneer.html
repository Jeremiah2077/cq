The Invitation

Someone has to be first. Why not you?

This summer, China Quest will send a hand-picked group of Irish students and teachers on the very first journey of its kind — a structured, curriculum-mapped immersion across Beijing, Xi'an, and Shanghai.

The whole trip is funded. In return, you travel with eyes open and a camera in your pocket. You share what you see — with your school, your family, and the hundreds of Irish students who'll follow in your footsteps.

Eligibility

Who we're looking for

For Students

Curious. Creative. Game.

  • →Transition Year, 5th Year, or 6th Year
  • →Attending a secondary school in the Republic of Ireland
  • →Valid passport (or able to obtain one before departure)
  • →Genuine curiosity about China — not just a free trip
  • →Willing to create content and share your experience back home
For Teachers

Subject leaders. Trip-ready.

  • →Registered with the Teaching Council of Ireland
  • →Currently teaching at a participating school
  • →Relevant subject preferred — History, Geography, CSPE, Art, Business, Chinese Studies
  • →Principal's recommendation
  • →Ready to champion future travel from your school

Selection is based on merit — not family income. We welcome applicants from fee-paying and non-fee-paying schools alike, and will balance our final cohort across at least three of Ireland's four provinces.

Your Role

What we ask in return

A Pioneer place is a gift with a role attached. You're not a tourist — you're part of the team.

01

Document the journey

One short video or a handful of captioned photos each day. You capture. We help you tell the story.

02

Bring it home

Give a short talk at your school when you're back. We'll give you a template — your job is to tell it your way.

03

Share honest feedback

A survey and a 30-minute debrief. What worked, what didn't, what would have made it better. We're listening.

04

A written reflection

100–200 words in your own voice. We might quote you — with your permission — in how we talk about what we do.

How to Apply

Four steps. About an hour of your time.

01

Register your interest

Sign up at the TY Show on 28 April, or online once the form goes live. Takes two minutes.

02

Send us a 60–90 second video

Filmed on your phone. The prompt: "Why I want to go to China." We're not looking for polish — we're looking for you.

03

Write a 200-word statement

Tell us what you'd bring back — to your classmates, your family, your community. Short is fine. Honest is better.

04

Principal + parent sign-off

A short recommendation from your principal, and a signed consent form from a parent or guardian. Templates provided — no fuss.

Questions, Answered

Things we hear a lot

Is this really free? What's the catch? +

Yes — flights, accommodation, meals, activities, visa and insurance are all covered. In return, you commit to documenting your journey, sharing what you've learnt, and giving us honest feedback. That's the exchange.

Do I need to speak Mandarin? +

No. Our team includes bilingual leaders and local licensed guides. You'll pick up a few phrases — dumplings require negotiation — but no prior Mandarin is required.

What if my school isn't one of the first participating schools? +

Any secondary school in the Republic of Ireland can put forward a student or teacher. We aim to balance the cohort across 4–5 schools to keep the peer dynamic strong and geographically varied.

What happens if I'm not selected? +

We keep 5 candidates on a waiting list in case a Pioneer has to drop out. And when future China Quest trips open up, all applicants are first in line for information and priority places.

How do you keep students safe on the ground? +

Garda-vetted Irish leader with the group at all times, local licensed guides, 24/7 emergency contact for parents, full insurance, mapped hospitals at every stop, and a daily check-in routine. Our full safety policy has the detail.

Who runs China Quest? +

China Quest is operated by Miles Minds Limited, an Ireland-based education company founded in 2005. Our team is Irish and China-based, working together to build structured learning journeys.
