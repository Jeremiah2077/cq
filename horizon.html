Day by Day

Full Itinerary

Every day is structured around the Explore → Reflect → Challenge → Circle learning cycle. Click any day to expand.

1
Dublin
Dublin → Chengdu — Depart
  • Pre-departure briefing at Dublin Airport
  • Overnight flight to Chengdu (connection via European hub)
  • China Quest Taskbook distributed — pre-arrival activities
2
Chengdu
Arrive & Jinli Ancient Street
  • Arrive Chengdu — hotel check-in and group orientation
  • Evening: Jinli Ancient Street — Shu culture, street food, local crafts
  • Evening debrief: acclimatisation and first impressions

Learning focus: Acclimatisation, Shu culture and the identity of Sichuan province.

3
Chengdu
Panda Keeper Program & Ancient Irrigation (UNESCO)
  • Morning: Panda Keeper Program (half-day) — feed pandas, clean enclosures, and assist with behavioural observation alongside sanctuary staff
  • Afternoon: Dujiangyan Irrigation System (UNESCO World Heritage Site) — 2,000-year-old hydraulic engineering still in use today
  • Taskbook entry: sketching and labelling the Dujiangyan system from observation

Curriculum link: LC Biology — conservation, biodiversity, population genetics; LC Geography — hydraulic engineering, sustainable water management.

4
Chengdu → Guilin
Fly to Guilin + Karst Geology Workshop
  • Morning: fly Chengdu → Guilin (approx. 1.5 hours)
  • Afternoon: Reed Flute Cave — guided karst geology workshop; stalactite and stalagmite formation, limestone solution processes
  • Evening: orientation along the Li River waterfront

Curriculum link: LC Earth Science / Geography — karst formation, limestone dissolution, cave systems.

5
Longji
Rice Terraces Ecosystem Workshop & Zhuang Village Homestay
  • Travel to Longji Rice Terraces — ecosystem workshop on altitude, microclimate, and sustainable agriculture
  • Rice planting practical — hands-on participation with local Zhuang farmers
  • Overnight: Zhuang village homestay — family meals, local custom, community life

Curriculum link: LC Geography — sustainable agriculture, SDGs 2 (Zero Hunger) and 15 (Life on Land); Sociology — indigenous minority communities in China.

6
Longji / Guilin
Community Service & Cultural Exchange
  • Morning service project: trail maintenance or school garden build — hands-on community contribution
  • Afternoon: Zhuang and Yao cultural exchange — batik dyeing workshop and bamboo weaving with village artisans
  • Evening: reflection circle — service, reciprocity, and what it means to help

Curriculum link: TY Service-Learning module; LC Art & Design — ethnic minority craft traditions (batik, bamboo); SDG 11 (Sustainable Communities).

7
Guilin → Yangshuo
Li River Cruise & Countryside Cycling
  • Morning: Li River cruise (Guilin → Yangshuo, approx. 4 hours) — students complete geology field notes as they drift past karst peaks
  • Afternoon: countryside cycling through rice paddies and rural villages surrounding Yangshuo
  • Evening debrief: comparing fluvial landscapes and human land use along the Li River corridor

Curriculum link: LC Geography — fluvial geomorphology, karst landscapes, rural land use; PE — endurance and teamwork.

8
Yangshuo
Farm-to-Table Cooking Class & Adventure Activities
  • Morning: farm-to-table cooking class — visit market, select seasonal produce, cook Guilin dishes with local chef
  • Afternoon: student choice — rock climbing on Yangshuo karst cliffs or kayaking on the Yulong River
  • Evening: reflection journal — food systems, local ecology, and SDG connections

Curriculum link: Sustainability — food systems, SDG 12 (Responsible Consumption); Geography — local ecology; teamwork and personal challenge.

9
Zhangjiajie
Avatar Mountains & Golden Whip Stream
  • Morning: fly Guilin → Zhangjiajie; transfer to national park
  • Afternoon: Zhangjiajie National Forest Park — Avatar Hallelujah Mountain; understand the real geology behind the film's floating mountains
  • Late afternoon: Golden Whip Stream hike — 7.5km trail through old-growth forest and towering sandstone pillars

Curriculum link: LC Geology / Geography — sandstone pillar formation, tectonic uplift, erosion; Film & Media — how landscape inspires creative storytelling.

10
Zhangjiajie
Tianmen Mountain & Grand Canyon Glass Bridge
  • Morning: Tianmen Mountain — world's longest cable car ascent; glass-floor sky walkway; Tianmen Cave (natural arch)
  • Afternoon: Zhangjiajie Grand Canyon Glass Bridge — longest and highest glass bridge globally; structural engineering discussion
  • Evening: final group reflection — landscapes, scale, and our relationship with the natural world

Curriculum link: LC Physics — materials, forces, glass under load; Engineering — cable systems, suspension structures.

11
Zhangjiajie → Changsha → Dublin
Closing Reflection & Depart
  • Morning: group closing reflection circle — key learning moments, changed perceptions, post-trip commitments
  • Taskbook final entry: "What in this landscape will stay with you, and why?"
  • Transfer to Changsha airport — depart for Dublin (connection via hub)
12
Dublin
Arrive Dublin
  • Arrive Dublin Airport — met by school / parents
  • Post-trip impact report sent to school within 10 working days
  • Parent information evening materials and TY portfolio certificate dispatched
